About the Epistorm Ensemble

Project Overview

The Epistorm Ensemble Flu Forecasting Dashboard provides weekly forecasts of influenza-associated hospital admissions across the United States. The ensemble combines predictions from multiple individual forecasting models to produce robust probabilistic forecasts of hospitalization trends.

Ensemble Methodology

Two ensemble methods are used:

  • Median Ensemble — Combines forecasts by taking the median across individual model quantiles at each prediction interval level.
  • LOP (Linear Opinion Pool) Ensemble — Combines full probability distributions from each model, often producing smoother and wider prediction intervals.

Component Models

The ensemble includes forecasts from the following individual models:

  • MIGHTE-Nsemble
  • MIGHTE-Joint
  • CEPH-Rtrend_fluH
  • MOBS-EpyStrain_Flu
  • MOBS-GLEAM_RL_FLUH
  • NU-PGF_FLUH
  • NEU_ISI-FluBcast
  • NEU_ISI-AdaptiveEnsemble
  • Gatech-ensemble_prob
  • Gatech-ensemble_stat

Activity Levels

Activity levels are determined by comparing current hospitalization rates to historical thresholds set by the CDC. Levels range from Low to Very High based on the number of weekly hospital admissions per location.

Trend Forecasts

Trend categories describe the expected direction and magnitude of change in hospitalizations over the forecast horizon. They range from Large Decrease to Large Increase, helping public health officials anticipate surges or declines.

Prediction Intervals

Prediction intervals show the range of likely outcomes for hospitalization forecasts. The 50% interval (darker shaded band) contains the middle half of predictions, while the 95% interval (lighter band) captures nearly all expected outcomes. Wider intervals indicate greater uncertainty in the forecast.

Data Sources

Observed hospitalization data is sourced from the CDC's National Healthcare Safety Network (NHSN). Activity level thresholds are based on CDC-established benchmarks for influenza hospitalization rates.

Evaluation Metrics

Weighted Interval Score (WIS) measures the accuracy and calibration of probabilistic forecasts. The WIS ratio compares each model's score to the FluSight-baseline — values below 1 indicate better-than-baseline performance.

Prediction Interval Coverage measures the proportion of observed values falling within each prediction interval. Well-calibrated models should have coverage close to the nominal interval level (e.g., a 90% PI should contain 90% of observations).
